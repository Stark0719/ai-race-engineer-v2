Undercut Strategy:
An undercut occurs when a driver pits earlier than a competitor and uses fresh tyres to gain lap time advantage. It is most effective when tyre degradation is high and pit delta is low.

Overcut Strategy:
An overcut involves extending a stint while competitors pit. It is effective when tyre degradation is low and track position is valuable.

Safety Car Pit Delta:
During a safety car period, the time lost during a pit stop is significantly reduced due to slower field pace.

Tyre Warmup:
New tyres require 1–2 laps to reach optimal operating temperature. Initial laps may be slower.

Tyre Cliff:
Tyres may experience nonlinear degradation near end of life, resulting in sudden lap time loss.
